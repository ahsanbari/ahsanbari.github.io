Rapping up Counter Hegemony

History

For the last four decades, rap has served as a medium for disenfranchised groups to spread ideologies that convey messages that challenged the existing social structure. The War on Drugs created a hegemonic ideology that Black men were criminals. After the effects of the war on drugs and the mass incarceration of African American men began, there was a significant shift in the theme, lyrics, and melody of most rap songs from upbeat, funk rap to a more cynical, gangsta rap. In 1979 The Sugarhill Gang released “Rapper’s Delight”, the first commercially successful rap song. Rapper’s Delight was an upbeat rap song that addressed the benefits of being a rapper. In less than 10 years, there was a dramatic shift in the direction rap music was heading.

Rap in Contemporary Society

Since the beginning of the 21st century, rappers have challenged many issues including police brutality, mass incarceration, and politics. Rappers such as Kendrick Lamar, The Roots, Nas, Joey Bada$$, Lupe Fiasco, The Game, and J. Cole have continued to discuss cultural and social issues in their music. From album cover art to lyrics and album titles, these artists have encouraged social change and influence.
